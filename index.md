# Avery Yen

Avery Yen is an AI researcher at Northeastern studying how language models reason, adapt, and fail under real-world constraints.

## Research interests

- Evaluation for small and mid-sized reasoning models
- Tool use, retrieval, and grounded generation
- Human feedback loops for reliable model behavior

## Current work

I spend most of my time designing benchmarks, tracing failure modes, and building lightweight research prototypes that turn vague model behavior into something measurable.

When I write here, it is usually about experiments, interface decisions, and what breaks once a model leaves the benchmark.
